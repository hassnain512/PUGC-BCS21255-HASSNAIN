Law Department

The Law department offers programs in legal studies, focusing on constitutional law, criminal justice, civil rights, and international law, preparing students for careers in legal practice or public service. The curriculum is designed to build a strong foundation in legal reasoning, research, and advocacy skills, equipping students to navigate complex legal systems and effectively represent clients or causes. In addition to core legal subjects, students engage with case studies, moot court competitions, and internships, providing practical exposure to real-world legal challenges. The department emphasizes ethical conduct and professionalism, ensuring that graduates are not only knowledgeable in law but also committed to justice and societal well-being. This well-rounded education prepares students for roles in litigation, corporate law, human rights, or government service, and for further specialization through advanced legal studies.

Bachelor in Law

The Bachelor of Laws (LLB) program is designed to provide students with a comprehensive understanding of legal principles, judicial processes, and the justice system. It emphasizes critical thinking, legal reasoning, and research skills necessary for effective legal practice. The program covers a broad range of legal disciplines, including constitutional law, criminal law, and international law, while promoting ethical conduct and professional responsibility. Graduates are well-prepared for careers in legal practice, public service, or further studies in law and justice.
